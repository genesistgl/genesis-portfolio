A motorcycle helmet is a crucial piece of safety equipment designed to protect the rider's head in the event of an accident. Helmets are typically made from high-quality, durable materials such as polycarbonate, fiberglass, or carbon fiber. The primary function of a motorcycle helmet is to protect the rider's head from impact and injury in the event of an accident.

The helmet's outer shell is designed to absorb and distribute the force of impact, while the inner lining provides cushioning and helps to prevent head injuries such as concussions. Many helmets also feature additional safety features such as chin straps and visors to further protect the rider's face and neck.
