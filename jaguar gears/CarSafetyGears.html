A blind spot detector, also known as a blind spot monitoring system, is a safety feature found on many modern vehicles. The system uses sensors and cameras to detect other vehicles or objects in the driver's blind spots, which are areas that cannot be seen using the side mirrors or rearview mirror.

When another vehicle is detected in the blind spot, the system will typically alert the driver through a visual or audible warning, such as a flashing light or beeping sound. This warning helps to prevent accidents by alerting the driver to the presence of another vehicle before they attempt to change lanes or make a turn.

A parking device is a piece of technology designed to help drivers park their vehicles safely and accurately. These devices can take many forms, from simple parking sensors to more advanced systems that provide visual and audible feedback to the driver.

One of the most common types of parking devices is a parking sensor system. These systems use sensors located on the vehicle's bumper to detect obstacles and provide feedback to the driver through visual and audible warnings. As the driver gets closer to an obstacle, the sensors will beep more rapidly, warning the driver to stop before hitting the obstacle.

A head restraint, also known as a headrest, is a safety feature found on many modern vehicles. The head restraint is located on the top of the seatback and is designed to prevent whiplash injuries in the event of a rear-end collision.
